For teachers

Julia Tools

JuliaTools allows teachers to quickly create perfect random ideally-sized groupings. Supports multiple sections/classes and absences.

My wife is a public school teacher and has been asking me to make her software for years. This year, I learned that she had a specific way of creating groups for students that none of her other software could get seamlessly.

I spent time listening to her algorithm, and them implemented it in a basic Python script. After validating it's success, I created a Python Django webapp around it.

For focus

Priority Todo

Clean, simple todo system focused on scoping achievements.

A friend in an executive position once mentioned that an effective day was getting at least three things done.

This applies his effective-day definition to a browser-based todo list. You have a max of 6 items, reprioritizable. When you complete 3, you get a success notification!

I built and used an initial prototype in Google Sheets, then built the webapp in mostly vanilla CSS and JS with a little jQuery. Uses local storage for persistence.

For celebrations

Digi Dreidel

Play a realtime 3D multi-player dreidel game while world's apart.

In the beginnings of the Covid pandemic, our family Chanukah party was cancelled indeterminately. To help people celebrate virtually, I wanted to create a digital dreidel game.

The prototype was a single HTML page with a 3D dreidel powered by three.js and unlimited spins.

This year, I began working on a true multi-player, multi-browser, turn-based version (and bought the domain digidreidel.com).
